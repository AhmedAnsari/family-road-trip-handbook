◆ Watch for
  • Cold water. Manzanita Lake is snowmelt-fed — stays cold even in summer. Skip more than a brief dip for the 9-month-old; bring a properly fitted infant life vest if she's on a boat at all.

◆ Watch for
  • ~8 hours total driving today. The heaviest day of the trip — consider trimming the Klamath Falls stop to build in slack.
  • Cold & wind at the rim. Elevation runs 7,000–8,000ft. Pack jackets for everyone, even in summer.
  • Highway 62 curves. The final climb to the rim has sweeping turns — keep ginger chews or snacks on hand if the kids get carsick.
  • Car seat breaks. Aim for a stretch/diaper/feed stop at least every 2 hours for the baby.
2026 update: the park's east-side roads have active construction and Cleetwood Cove Trail is closed through 2028 — neither affects the West Rim overlooks in this plan, but check the NPS conditions page the morning you go.

◆ Watch for
  • Waterfall footing. Overlooks are paved and easy, but trails down to the falls have wet rock, stairs, and no railings in spots — stick to the overlooks with the 4-year-old on foot.
  • ~6.5 hrs driving before the 4.5-hr drive home. The longest day end-to-end — time the final drive to overlap with the baby's nap if you can.
  • Lake Siskiyou is the easy one. Beach access and calmer water — the best-suited stop of the whole trip for both kids.
Quick reference

Traveling with a 9-month-old & a 4-year-old

Altitude

Both Lassen (~8,200ft) and Crater Lake (~7,100ft) sit well above where most babies spend their time. Healthy infants generally do fine, but check with your pediatrician beforehand and watch for unusual fussiness once you're up there.

Cold water

Manzanita Lake and the Shasta-area lakes are snowmelt-fed and stay cold in summer. Keep the baby's time in the water brief, and bring a properly fitted infant life vest if she'll be on a boat.

Car seat time

Day 2 runs close to 8 hours of driving; Day 3 pairs 6.5 hours of stop-to-stop driving with a 4.5-hour drive home. Build in a stretch/diaper/feed break at least every 2 hours.

Sun & layers

Elevation means stronger UV than it feels. Pack jackets for the Lassen and Crater Lake rim stops, plus hats and sunscreen for every outdoor stop.
