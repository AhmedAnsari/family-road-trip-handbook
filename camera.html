Section 02

Core Concepts — What Each Setting Actually Does

The Exposure Triangle

Three settings, all trading off against each other to control how bright the photo is.

SettingControlsSide effect of going higher
ISOSensor sensitivityMore grain (noise)
Aperture (f/)Lens opening size — lower f/ = more lightLess in focus (shallower depth)
ShutterHow long sensor sees lightMotion blur if too slow

Dial Modes on the T7

You only need four — ignore the rest.

Tv — you pick shutter, camera picks aperture. Use for motion. Av — you pick aperture, camera picks shutter. Use for landscapes & portraits. M — you pick everything. Use for night/stars. P — camera picks both. Use when something's happening fast and you can't think.

AF Modes Explained

Two modes matter for this trip:

ModeWhen to use
One ShotStationary subjects — landscapes, still portraits, waterfalls. Locks focus when you half-press and stays locked.
AI ServoMoving subjects — kids running, animals, anything unpredictable. Camera tracks continuously while you hold the shutter half-pressed.

Image Stabilization (IS)

The kit lens has IS built in. Simple rules:

SituationIS Setting
Handheld — anythingOn, Mode 1
On a tripodOff — IS detects fake movement and adds blur
Panning (tracking a moving subject)On, Mode 2 (if available on your firmware)

RAW vs JPEG

RAW saves all sensor data — more editing latitude for exposure and white balance in Lightroom. JPEG is smaller and processed in-camera. For this trip:

FormatBest for
RAWLandscapes, night sky, anything you plan to edit or print
JPEG L (Fine)Quick share, kid burst shots where you'll pick 3 of 20
RAW+JPEGMoving kids — burst in JPEG, keep the winners as RAW

Exposure Compensation (+/−)

The quickest fix in Av or Tv mode. Press the Av +/− button and spin the wheel.

SituationTry
Subject looks dark against bright sky+1/3 to +1
Sky is blown white or very bright−1/3 to −2/3
Snow or white sand looks grey+1 to +2
Night / low light scene looks washed−1

Pre-Shoot Checklist

  • Battery charged (and spare battery in bag)
  • SD card formatted and empty
  • Lens cap off — IS on
  • Image quality set to RAW (or RAW+JPEG for action)
  • Date & time correct for geo-tagging
  • Diopter eyepiece adjusted for your eye
  • Auto power-off set to 1–2 min (saves battery)
  • Custom function: Mirror lockup for tripod shots
  • Sensor clean check — look at clear blue sky at f/16
  • Lens clean — check front element for smudges
  • Test shot and histogram review before starting
  • Flash popped down (unless needed)
